Getting around

Transportation deserves its own page.

Reviewer feedback consistently pointed to the same issue: travelers did not want transportation buried beneath attractions and lodging.

Most visitors arrive by air.

Taniti is served by a small airport for small jets and propeller planes. Some visitors also arrive by a cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay one night per week.

  • Air travel is the main arrival method.
  • Cruise arrivals matter, but they are a secondary path.
  • This is why air-related planning is easy to find first in the prototype.

Choose transportation based on your trip style.

Public buses run in Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. Private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis, rental cars, and bike rentals are also available.

  • Rental car: useful for families or self-directed explorers
  • Taxi or shuttle: easier for shorter stays
  • Bike rental: better in flatter, more walkable areas

Some areas are easier on foot than others.

Taniti City is fairly flat and walkable. Many tourists also stay near Merriton Landing, which is easy to explore on foot once you are there.

  • Useful for first-time visitors who want fewer moving pieces
  • Supports shorter park-once, walk-more trips
  • Good pairing with hotel or resort stays

Practical details should not be hidden.

This FAQ section was added because users wanted easy answers to the practical questions that usually come up right before booking.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen, several banks offer currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.
Violent crime is very rare, but petty crime such as pickpocketing has increased as tourism has grown. There is one hospital and several clinics, and the hospital includes many multilingual employees.
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.
Yes. Taniti observes many national holidays, and tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed on those days.
Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is 18.

Five objective prototype tasks

These are written as specific find-or-complete tasks so later testing can focus on usability instead of personal preference alone.

1

Find the best section to learn how a visitor can travel from the airport to other parts of the island.

2

Locate one lodging option that would work well for a couple and one that would work well for a family group.

3

Determine whether Taniti uses the U.S. dollar and whether major credit cards are accepted.

4

Find one rainforest activity and one activity near Yellow Leaf Bay or Merriton Landing.

5

Find where the site explains whether holiday closures could affect restaurant or attraction plans.
